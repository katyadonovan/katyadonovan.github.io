Heat Treating Titanium

Tempering, Annealing, and Quenching Pure Titanium and Titanium Alloy

For my final project for my Materials Science class, my team explored different ways of heat treating commercially pure (CP) Titanium and Titanium 6Al-4V alloy. We quickly learned that some form of heat treatments do not work particularly well with Titanium, but they all changed in an expected way. After annealing, quenching, and aging our Titanium samples, we performed tensile testing using an Instron, we analyzed the fracture using a Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM) , tested the hardness of the samples, determined the element composition using energy dispersive x-ray spectroscopy (EDS), and examined the grain structure before and after the heat treatments. All this data was collected and analyzed, until we had a cohesive explanation for how each heat treatment affected the samples.
