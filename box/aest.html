Appropriate Energy Saving Technologies Internship

Exploring Sustainable Carbonization Processes

Appropriate Energy Savings Technology is a woman-led social enterprise that creates livelihood opportunities for women in the cook stove and fuel value chain in Eastern Uganda. It was created as a response to the limited amount of firewood in Uganda and works on making charcoal from sustainable processes.

During my internship, I worked with MITâ€™s Development Lab to create and research different carbonization processes of food waste. The previous method that AEST was using was burning groundnut shells and then extinguishing the fire once the shells were carbonized. Unfortunately, this was a very unsafe process for the workers due to all the smoke, as well as time consuming. The carbonization processes another student and I created, inspired by Thai method for carbonizing rice husks, burned the groundnut shells cleaner and much faster.
