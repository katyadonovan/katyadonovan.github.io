Analysis of a Bike Frame

FEA Analysis Validated with Strain Gauge Testing

As our final project for our Mechanics of Structures and Solids class, my partner and I decided to analyze the stress on a specific bike frame as it goes over bumps using strain gauges and compare it to our finite element analysis.

To calculate the stress experienced in an actual bicycle frame we super glued four strain gauges to the top tube, seat stay, and fork of the bicycle. We then collected data from the strain gauges when the bike was loaded with a rider along a flat surface for calibration. We also collected data when the rider road the bicycle over a front bump and back bump.

In order to verify our results, we compared our calculations to a study using Finite Element Analysis (FEA) after replicating the model of the bike in Solidworks. There were some discrepancies between Solidworks FEA and our strain gauge data, but the areas of highest and lowest stress were the same.
